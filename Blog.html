Brain Tumor Detection using CNN

Brain tumor detection using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) involves developing a machine learning model that can accurately identify and classify brain tumors from medical imaging, such as MRI scans. The process begins with gathering a dataset of brain images, which is then preprocessed to enhance image quality and ensure consistency. The CNN, a type of deep learning model particularly effective for image recognition tasks, is trained on this dataset. During training, the CNN learns to identify patterns and features that distinguish tumors from normal brain tissue. Once trained, the model can analyze new MRI scans, providing automated, accurate, and rapid detection of brain tumors, which aids in early diagnosis and treatment planning.

Prediction of Employee Attrition

Prediction of employee attrition using machine learning (ML) algorithms involves building a model to foresee which employees are likely to leave a company. This process begins with collecting and preprocessing relevant employee data, such as demographics, job satisfaction, performance metrics, and historical attrition records. Various ML algorithms, such as logistic regression, decision trees, random forests, and gradient boosting, are then trained on this data to identify patterns and factors contributing to employee turnover. The model learns to recognize key indicators of attrition and can predict the likelihood of current employees leaving. This predictive insight allows organizations to proactively address issues, enhance employee retention strategies, and make informed decisions to maintain a stable and motivated workforce.
